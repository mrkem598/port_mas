About

Mission and Vision

The Empower Project aims to bridge the gap between trained workforce and job search primarily in the DMV area and will expand gradually to the other parts of the city and the world. The program dedicated to providing mental training and workshop to empower graduated and near graduation students. The program designed to prepare students for their first job by boosting their confidence, showing professional clues, and tips to succeed in an interview. The Empower Project mission is to empower young adults primarily for girls to get their job by providing well selected technical resources available over the internet. This project mostly aims to solve problems associated with the lack of experience which is entirely required by the recruiter. The Empower Project offers these services virtually and also by having the live workshop in Washington D.C. The seminars will be a base for interacting with others, creating connections, and learning from experienced professionals how they tackle such challenging issues in their career. In each seminar, there will be invited speakers including government officials. The goal of Empower Project is to provide individually curated training resources to get the required skills and knowledge to succeed in a technical interview. The project will guide how to use resources from anywhere to primarily empower in getting a job right after graduation or near graduation. With the continued success of Empower Project, more and more young adults will be attracted to using their smart devices to learn from the internet and build their capability.

The Purpose

The Empower Project was started to combat the grim circumstances facing graduate students finding the job in our communities. The graduate students face increasingly lack experience in finding employment and result in high unemployment rates, unable to repay financial aid, dependent in family or homelessness. Majority of a graduate student will also involve in an unprofessional job. In the face of those grim statistics, many of youth and young girls decide early in life to become a product of their environment and drop out of school. This often leads to criminal behavior, drug use, and other socially destructive activities. The Empower Project has embarked on a unique, virtually based to offer empowerment to young adults in our graduate student from the communities facing these problems. The Empower Project encourages young adults to embrace a rewarding career by providing tips and clue virtually and in a seminar. The Empower Project invented by a ServiceNow Star Mohammed Kemal who is talented, caring, professional. He designed a unique program, tailored to the specific needs of our graduate student youth and young adults. The Empower Project sees our graduate students as the next generation of leaders and mentors in our communities. The Empower Project is helping to break the barrier on how to use available online resources to get the required skills and encourages them to have pride in themselves, their accomplishments, build confidence, and self-esteem. The project will bring experienced professionals to share their success stories in how to get a first professional job right after graduation. Empower Project will launch in October 2018 and detail plan will be posted on the website. Empower project will empower young adults to get into professional jobs.
